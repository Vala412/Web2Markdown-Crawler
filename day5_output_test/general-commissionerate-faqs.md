### General Commissionerate FAQs

#### General Information

### What is the role of the Commissioner of Customs (General), ACC Mumbai Zone-III?

The Commissionerate oversees various administrative, legal, and operational functions related to customs. This includes managing officer postings, vigilance, investigations, legal matters, taxpayer services, and cargo clearance.

### How does the Commissionerate ensure smooth customs operations?

We focus on transparency, efficiency, and compliance with customs regulations to facilitate smooth trade operations while preventing fraud and ensuring legal compliance.

#### Customs Administration & Services

### Who manages officer postings and transfers?

The  **P&E (Personnel & Establishment) Section handles transfers, postings, and establishment-related matters for officers and staff.**

### How are complaints against customs officers handled?

The  **Vigilance Section** investigates complaints received from official sources and maintains vigilance records to ensure integrity and compliance.

### What measures are taken to improve service quality?

The  **Sevottam Cell** continuously evaluates internal processes, incorporates citizen feedback, and ensures effective implementation of the Citizen’s Charter to enhance service standards.

### How does the Commissionerate manage bank guarantees?

The  **BG (Bank Guarantee) Cell** keeps accurate records of bank guarantees for regulatory compliance.

### Who is responsible for maintaining official records?

The  **MCD (Management & Control of Documents) Section manages and maintains all official documents and records.**

#### Investigations & Compliance

### What happens if there is a customs violation?

The CIU (Customs Intelligence Unit) investigates cases related to import/export fraud, including misdeclaration, misclassification and undervaluation of goods where case has a vigilance aspect, and rest of the cases handled by concerned SIIB.

### How is confiscated or unclaimed cargo handled?

The  **Disposal Section** manages the disposal of confiscated, unclaimed, or uncleared cargo, as well as expired bonded goods, in accordance with customs regulations.

### Who oversees preventive customs operations?

The  **Preventive General Section** manages administrative work related to preventive officers and superintendents.

#### Trade Facilitation & Logistics

### Where can I find information about customs notices and tenders?

The **CHS Section** issues notices, tenders, corrigenda, and bid details while also organising official events and initiatives like Swachhta Pakhwada.

### Who manages customs-related transport services?

The  **MTO (Motor Transport Organisation) Section provides and manages vehicles for customs officers and staff.**

### What does the SEEPZ Section handle?

This section deals with customs-related matters concerning the  **Santacruz Electronics Export Processing Zone (SEEPZ).**

#### Cargo Clearance & Warehousing

### How are post-clearance audits conducted?

The  **PCA (Post-Clearance Audit) Section** verifies import declarations, country of origin, and duty benefits while conducting audits to ensure compliance.

### What is the purpose of the Bond Section?

The  **Bond Section** manages the warehousing of imported goods under Chapter 9 of the Customs Act, 1962

### How is the movement of transhipment cargo handled?

The  **Transhipment Section** approves and oversees the duty-free movement of goods under transhipment permits from the Air Cargo Complex to SEZs and foreign-bound vessels.

#### Legal, RTI & Taxpayer Assistance

### How can I request information under the RTI Act?

The  **RTI Section** processes applications submitted by Indian citizens, forwards them to relevant departments, and provides responses as per the  **Right to Information (RTI) Act.**

### Where can I get help regarding customs-related queries or issues?

The **Taxpayer Service Centre** assists with queries and grievances via e-helpline and telephone support.

### How are customs-related legal matters handled?

The Legal Section coordinates appeals, liaises with courts, appoints government counsel, and facilitates legal proceedings

### What does the Adjudication Section do?

This section assists customs authorities in resolving disputes by adjudicating cases and issuing final orders.
